[Civic Opera House (Chicago)](/wiki/Civic_Opera_House_(Chicago))Original ballet companyBallet Caravan CompanySetting[Wild West](/wiki/Wild_West)*Billy the Kid* is a 1938 [ballet](/wiki/Ballet) written by the American composer [Aaron Copland](/wiki/Aaron_Copland) on commission from [Lincoln Kirstein](/wiki/Lincoln_Kirstein). It was choreographed by [Eugene Loring](/wiki/Eugene_Loring) for [Ballet Caravan](/wiki/New_York_City_Ballet). Along with *Rodeo* and *Appalachian Spring*, it is one of Copland's most popular and widely performed pieces. It is most famous for its incorporation of several cowboy tunes and American folk songs and, although built around the figure and the exploits of [Billy the Kid](/wiki/Billy_the_Kid), is not so much a biography of a notorious but peculiarly appealing desperado as it is a perception of the "Wild West", in which a figure such as Billy played a vivid role.[[1]](#cite_note-1)

It premiered on 16 October 1938[[2]](#cite_note-2) in Chicago by the Ballet Caravan Company, with pianists [Arthur Gold](/wiki/Gold_and_Fizdale) and [Walter Hendl](/wiki/Walter_Hendl) performing a two-piano version of the score.  The first performance in New York City occurred on 24 May 1939, with an orchestra conducted by Fritz Kitzinger.

## Story

[[edit](/w/index.php?title=Billy_the_Kid_(ballet)&action=edit&section=1)]**Setting:** New Mexico and Arizona, 1870s and 1880s

The story follows the life of the infamous outlaw [Billy the Kid](/wiki/Billy_the_Kid). It begins with "The Open Prairie" and shows many pioneers trekking westward. The action shifts to a small frontier town, in which a young Billy and his mother are present. His mother is killed by a stray bullet during a gunfight and he stabs her killer, then goes on the run.

The next scene shows episodes in Billy's later life. He is living in the desert, is hunted and captured by a posse (in which the ensuing gun battle features prominent percussive effects) and taken to jail. He manages to escape after stealing a gun from the warden during a game of cards and returns to his hideout, where he thinks he is safe, but sheriff [Pat Garrett](/wiki/Pat_Garrett) catches up and shoots him to death. The ballet ends with "The Open Prairie" theme and pioneers once again travelling west.
